Quality of Life Around the World

This visualisation explores global quality of life indicators. It focuses on economic and social themes. This visualisation is designed to help students, researchers or merely curious viewers better understand how various factors interact across countries.

The data draws from reputable open sources such as the World Bank and OECD.

To make complex relationships obvious, various idioms are utilised to highlight geographic patterns and statistical corelations, making it easier for users to compare regions and uncover insights. I encourage the reader to ask themselves "In what regard is living in Australia better than some other country that is often idolised?". Maybe even compare it to your home country if applicable! Interactive features such as filters and tooltips allow for these personalised explorations/insights. Have fun!

Global Overview

The map highlights worldwide differences in quality of life, providing the foundation for deeper analysis. Minimum wage numbers here have been adjusted for each country's cost of living. Did you know that Australia actually has the highest purchasing-power-adjusted minimum wage in the world? Another fun fact: Did you know that extremely developed nations like Singapore and Sweden actually have no legal minimum wage - it's entirely derived from the free market. Do you notice any trends between minimum wage and happiness levels of a country?

Comparative Metrics

These two charts complement each other: the parallel coordinate reveals broad multi-variable relationships across countries, while the radar chart illustrates detailed profiles for selected cases.

Socioeconomic Breakdown

The bar chart breaks down socioeconomic factors, showing which elements contribute most strongly to differences in quality of life across regions.

Government Expenditure & Immigration

The bubble chart shows how government spending relates to immigration, suggesting that stronger welfare states attract more newcomers.

Migration Trends Over Time

The bump chart illustrates changes in immigration flows, providing a dynamic view of how migration patterns evolve.

Tourism and Well-being

Tourism provides another lens on quality of life. Cultural richness, environment, and openness all shape both perceptions and lived experiences of well-being.
